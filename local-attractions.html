Explore Sahibganj

A Curated Guide to the Region's Treasures

Sacred & Spiritual Sites

Bindabasini Temple

A revered Shaktipeeth, this ancient temple complex is dedicated to the divine feminine trinity. Revived to prominence by the sage Pahari Baba, it is a site of profound spiritual significance and pilgrimage.

  • One of the ancient Shaktipeeths.
  • Dedicated to the Tridevi.
  • Holds deep connections to the mythology of Goddess Sati.
Approx. 1 hr 49 min drive (73 km)

Shivgadi Temple

Nestled within a natural cave, this unique temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, distinguished by a continuous, natural stream of water that drips from the cave ceiling directly onto the sacred Shiva Lingam.

  • Located inside a mountain cave.
  • Features a naturally occurring water source over the lingam.
  • A major pilgrimage site during the month of Shravan.
Approx. 1 hr 24 min drive (58 km)

Echoes of History

Akbari Masjid

Step into the Mughal era at the Akbari Masjid, one of the region's oldest mosques, constructed during the reign of Emperor Akbar. This historic structure is a fine example of early Mughal architecture blended with regional influences.

  • Dates back to the 16th-century Mughal period.
  • Represents a blend of imperial and local architectural styles.
  • A site of significant historical and cultural importance.
Approx. 1 hr 13 min drive (37 km)

Singhi Dalan

The "Hall of Stone" is one of the most prominent remaining structures of the grand palace built by Sultan Shah Shuja in the 17th century. This imposing gateway served as the eastern entrance to the palace when Rajmahal was the capital of Bengal.

  • Constructed in the mid-17th century.
  • Part of Sultan Shah Shuja's former palace complex.
  • A testament to Rajmahal's history as a Mughal capital.
Approx. 1 hr 14 min drive (37 km)

Baradari

Once the residence of a confidant of Emperor Akbar, the Baradari is a historic palace named for its distinctive "twelve doors." Situated on the serene banks of the Ganges, its architectural elegance is preserved by the Archaeological Survey of India.

  • Features a unique twelve-door architectural design.
  • Offers picturesque views complemented by the Ganges.
  • Preserved as a site of national importance.
Approx. 1 hr 3 min drive (30 km)

Natural Wonders

Moti Jharna Waterfall

A breathtaking natural spectacle, Moti Jharna cascades over two rock ledges from a height of 50-60 feet. Nestled within the lush Rajmahal hills, this scenic waterfall is a popular destination for nature lovers seeking tranquility and picturesque beauty.

  • A significant waterfall in the Rajmahal hills.
  • Surrounded by a verdant, picturesque glen.
  • Features a nearby temple, blending nature with spirituality.
Approx. 34 min drive (14 km)

Sahibganj Steamer Ghat

This historic river port on the banks of the Ganges is more than just a transportation hub; it's the lifeline of the city. Offering serene views of the river, the ghat serves as a crucial ferry link to Bihar and remains a center of local trade, bustling with authentic regional character.

  • A vital river crossing point between Jharkhand and Bihar.
  • Offers scenic views and a glimpse into local commerce.
  • Holds historical significance from the British era.
Approx. 5 min drive (1.4 km)

Local Hubs & Landmarks

Sahibganj Port (MMT)

India's second riverine Multi-Modal Terminal is a modern marvel of engineering and a cornerstone of regional trade. Part of the national Jal Marg Vikas Project, this port seamlessly connects waterways, railways, and roadways.

  • A key inland waterway port on the National Waterway-1.
  • Facilitates seamless multi-modal cargo transport.
  • Crucial for the economic development of the region.
Approx. 30 min drive (10 km)

Sahibganj Railway Station

A key railway hub in the Eastern Railway Zone, Sahibganj Station (SBG) is a vital link on the main line connecting Howrah and Delhi. It offers excellent connectivity to major metropolitan cities, making it a crucial gateway for travelers.

  • Station Code: SBG
  • Operates under the Eastern Railway Zone.
  • Connects major cities like Kolkata, Delhi, and Patna.
Approx. 7 min drive (750 m)

Chowk Bazar

Experience the authentic pulse of the city at Chowk Bazar, a vibrant and bustling marketplace in the heart of Sahibganj. This central hub for shopping and cultural activities offers a rich blend of traditional charm and modern convenience.

  • The central marketplace of Sahibganj.
  • A vibrant hub for shopping and local culture.
  • Reflects the rich heritage of the region.
Approx. 1 min walk (290 m)
