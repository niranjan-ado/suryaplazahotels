Explore Sahibganj

A Curated Guide to the Region's Treasures

Sacred Sites History Natural Wonders Local Hubs

Sacred & Spiritual Sites

Bindabasini Temple

A revered Shaktipeeth, this ancient temple complex is dedicated to the divine feminine trinity. Revived to prominence by the sage Pahari Baba, it is a site of profound spiritual significance.

  • One of the ancient Shaktipeeths
  • Dedicated to the Tridevi
  • Holds deep connections to mythology
Approx. 1 hr 49 min drive (73 km)

Shivgadi Temple

Nestled within a natural cave, this unique temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, distinguished by a continuous, natural stream of water that drips from the cave ceiling onto the sacred Shiva Lingam.

  • Located inside a mountain cave
  • Natural water source over the lingam
  • Major pilgrimage site in Shravan
Approx. 1 hr 24 min drive (58 km)

Echoes of History

Akbari Masjid

Step into the Mughal era at one of the region's oldest mosques, constructed during the reign of Emperor Akbar. It is a fine example of early Mughal architecture blended with regional influences.

  • Dates back to the 16th-century
  • Blend of imperial and local styles
  • Site of historical importance
Approx. 1 hr 13 min drive (37 km)

Singhi Dalan

The "Hall of Stone" is a prominent structure of the grand palace built by Sultan Shah Shuja in the 17th century, when Rajmahal was the capital of Bengal.

  • Constructed in the mid-17th century
  • Part of a former palace complex
  • A testament to Mughal history
Approx. 1 hr 14 min drive (37 km)

Natural Wonders

Moti Jharna Waterfall

A breathtaking natural spectacle, Moti Jharna cascades from a height of 50-60 feet. Nestled within the lush Rajmahal hills, this scenic waterfall is a popular destination for nature lovers.

  • Significant waterfall in Rajmahal hills
  • Surrounded by a verdant glen
  • Blends nature with spirituality
Approx. 34 min drive (14 km)

Sahibganj Steamer Ghat

This historic river port on the banks of the Ganges is the lifeline of the city. Offering serene views, the ghat serves as a crucial ferry link to Bihar and remains a center of local trade.

  • Vital river crossing point
  • Scenic views and local commerce
  • Historically significant
Approx. 5 min drive (1.4 km)

Local Hubs & Landmarks

Sahibganj Railway Station

A key railway hub in the Eastern Railway Zone, Sahibganj Station (SBG) is a vital link on the main line connecting Howrah and Delhi, offering excellent connectivity to major cities.

  • Station Code: SBG
  • Connects to Kolkata, Delhi, Patna
  • Crucial gateway for travelers
Approx. 7 min drive (750 m)

Chowk Bazar

Experience the authentic pulse of the city at Chowk Bazar, a vibrant marketplace in the heart of Sahibganj. This central hub for shopping offers a rich blend of traditional charm and modern convenience.

  • The central marketplace of the city
  • Vibrant hub for shopping
  • Reflects the region's rich heritage
Approx. 1 min walk (290 m)
